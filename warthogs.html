WARTHOG

Warthogs are pigs who live in open and semi-open habitats, even in quite arid regions, in sub-Saharan Africa. Although covered in bristly hairs, a warthog's body and head appear largely bare, from a distance, with only a crest of hair along the back and the tufts on the face and tail being obvious.

The male warthogs also have very prominent tusks, which reach a length of 10 to 25 inches (25 to 64 centimetres); females' tusks are always smaller.

Warthogs are largely herbivorous, but, like most suids, opportunistically eat invertebrates or small animals, even scavenging on carrion
